Our Departments

Cardiology

It is a specialized field of veterinary medicine that focuses on the diagnosis, treatment, and management of cardiovascular diseases in animals. Veterinary cardiologists are veterinarians who have completed additional training and certification in cardiology, allowing them to provide advanced care for animals with heart and cardiovascular issues.

Diagnostic techniques used in veterinary cardiology include electrocardiography (ECG), echocardiography, radiography, and blood tests to evaluate the heart and cardiovascular system of animals.

Nephrology

Nephrology is a branch of veterinary medicine that focuses on the diagnosis, treatment, and management of diseases and conditions of the kidneys and urinary system in animals. Kidney disease is a common problem in animals, particularly in older pets, and can have various causes such as infections, toxins, and genetic factors.
Common kidney conditions in animals include chronic kidney disease, acute kidney injury, urinary tract infections, and kidney stones. In addition to treating kidney diseases, veterinary nephrologists may also work closely with other specialists such as oncologists, endocrinologists

Osteology

Osteology is a branch of veterinary medicine that deals with the study of the structure, function, and diseases of bones and skeletal tissues in animals. It is a fundamental part of anatomy and physiology, and is essential for understanding how the skeletal system supports and protects the body, enables movement, and produces blood cells.

Pulmonology

Pulmonology is a specialized field of veterinary medicine that focuses on the diagnosis, treatment, and management of respiratory diseases and disorders in animals.Veterinary pulmonologists use various diagnostic tools and techniques to evaluate and diagnose respiratory conditions in animals, including pulmonary function testing, radiography, CT scans, and bronchoscopy.

Common respiratory conditions in animals include asthma, bronchitis, pneumonia, and lung cancer, among others.
